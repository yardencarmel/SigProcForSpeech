Deltas for arXiv / lower-tier venue publication
================================================

1. EVALUATION SCALE (critical)
   - Current: 3 sentences, 1 speaker pair.
   - Needed: 20-50 utterances from a standard benchmark (LibriSpeech test-clean,
     VCTK, or Seed-TTS test-en).
   - Multiple speaker pairs (at least 3-5, ideally cross-gender).
   - Error bars / confidence intervals on all metrics.

2. MOS / SUBJECTIVE EVALUATION (critical)
   - Replace "informal listening impressions" with a proper MOS study.
   - 10-15 listeners rating naturalness and style similarity on a 1-5 Likert scale.
   - Even a small-scale study would satisfy reviewers.

3. REPRODUCIBILITY DETAILS
   - Specify exact software versions (PyTorch, F5-TTS commit hash).
   - Hardware (GPU model, VRAM).
   - Wall-clock inference times per method (especially Method B's 2x cost).

4. DEDICATED RELATED WORK SECTION
   - Currently the intro doubles as related work.
   - Compare to other inference-time style transfer approaches:
     prompt-based emotion control, adapter-based methods, NANSY, GenerSpeech.
   - Position the contribution more clearly against the literature.

5. MEL SPECTROGRAM VISUALISATIONS
   - Show spectrograms of baseline vs. Method A vs. Method B outputs side-by-side.
   - Makes the style transfer effect visually concrete for readers.

6. STATISTICAL SIGNIFICANCE
   - With a larger eval set, report p-values or bootstrapped CIs for the SIM-B
     improvements (especially Method B's +0.087).

7. SECURITY SECTION EXPANSION
   - Currently theoretical only.
   - For a venue like AAAI or Interspeech, either expand with a proof-of-concept
     (gradient-optimised x_0) or trim to a paragraph.
   - Currently falls between the two.

8. HEBREW SECTION EXPANSION
   - Either expand with spectrograms of gibberish output and a proper OOV analysis,
     or fold into a single discussion paragraph.

9. VENUE SUGGESTIONS
   - Interspeech (speech-specific, accepts 4-page papers): good fit if eval is scaled up.
   - SLT / ASRU: workshop-style venues that accept exploratory work.
   - arXiv preprint: could go up with current content; the community would find the
     SDEdit-for-flow-matching adaptation and 3D conditioning discovery useful.
